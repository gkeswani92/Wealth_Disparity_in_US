Wealth disparty in US

Age-Quintiles-Wealth Distribution in different years

The 3 heat maps depict the wealth distribution in 2000, 2005 and 2011. As we can see, the wealth was distributed more evenly in 2000 than it is in 2011. Through the years, the situation of people in quintile 1 (poorest group) is becoming worse. while the fifth quintile (richest group) has accumulated a lot more wealth than it had back in 2000 . The ratio of mean to median net worth in the 5th quintile becomes worse on the west coast where the mean network is 3.5 times the median networth.

Mean net worth per quantile

Now that we have seen the mean distribution of wealth across the various quantiles and their age groups, lets zoom out to view the comparison of mean net worths of the various quantiles through 2000 to 2011. As can be seen, the lower quantiles were starting to get closer to the 5th quantile but after the recession in 2008, the 5th quantile have accumulated almost 80% of the wealth as compared to the 74% in 2000

Median and Mean net worth comparison by regions of US

The mean net worth and median net worth in quintile 2, 3 and 4 are quite close to each other. However, the fifth quitile's mean net worth on an average is more than two times the median net worth, which shows the fact that there are some really very rich people who have accumulated most of the wealth.

Wealth Distribution within Race Groups

Each race group have been divided equally into 5 quintiles, from the richest to the poorest. Each quintile contains 20% of the population of that race. The following graph descirbes the percentage of net worth owned by 5 equally divided groups within each race. As shown, the majority of the wealth is owned by the richest 20%, while the poorest 20% has a negative net worth.
